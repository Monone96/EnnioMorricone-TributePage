Here is a timeline of Ennio Morricone's life:

  • 1928 - Born in Rome, Italy on October 11.
  • 1940 - He bagen studying music at Rome's Conservatory of Santa Cecilia at the age of 12.
  • 1946 - Received trumpet diploma and began working professionally.
  • 1946 - Composed "Il Mattino."
  • 1952 - Received "Diploma in Instrumentation for Band."
  • 1953 - He made his first arrangement for a series of evening radio shows.
  • 1954 - Obtained a diploma in Composition under the composer Goffredo Petrassi
  • 1955 - Began to "ghost write" and arrange music for other, already established, film composers.
  • 1958 - He obtained a job at RAI as music assistant, but he resigned on his first day of work.
  • 1961 - His career as a film composer began with Luciano Salce's "Il Federale"
  • 1964 - Became famous with his first movie score for Sergio Leone's "A Fistful of Dollars."
  • 1965 - Composes movie score for Sergio Leone's "For a Few Dollars More."
  • 1965 - Entered the group of improvisation, "Nuova Consonanza."
  • 1966 - Composes film score for Sergio Leone's "The Good, The Bad, and The Ugly."
  • 1968 - Creates film score for Sergio Leone's "Once Upon a Time in the West."
  • 1971 - Composes movie score for Sergio Leone's "A Fistful of Dynamite."
  • 1979 - Nominated for his first Academy Award for Best Original Score for "Days of Heaven."
  • 1984 - He, along with other composers, founded the I.R.T.E.M. (Institute of Research for Musical Theatre) in Rome.
  • 1987 - Won his first Golden Globe (USA) for Best Original Score for "The Mission."
  • 1988 - Won his first Grammy for Best Album of Instrumental Original Score for "The Untouchables."
